Japanese fried tofu

揚げ出し豆腐

agedashidofu

Crispy deep-fried tofu if one of my favorites. Agedashi Tofu is a popular appetizer you can find at izakaya and Japanese restaurants. It requires deep-frying, but the process is easier than you think. It can easily be converted to be vegetarian and vegan-friendly.

There are a wide range of possible toppings for the fried tofu, making it flexible as well as cheap.

Ingredients

  • Firm or Medium-firm tofu
  • Potato starch
  • Panko
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Tsuyu sauce
  • Kewpi Mayo
  • Garnish (smoked pepper, shredded green onions, kastuobushi, shichimi togarashi, etc)

Instructions

  1. Drain the tofu for 15 minutes. Don’t drain too long if you want the soft velvety texture on the inside.
  2. While your tofu drains, pour some tsuyu into a small bowl, and prepare your breading. I like a ratio of 1-to-1 with the panko and potato starch.
  3. Cut the tofu block into 2 laterally, then cut pieces (roughly 1.5-2 inch squares)
  4. dip pieces into the tsuyu then and coat them with breading
  5. Deep fry the tofu for 2 minutes until lightly browned and drain the excess oil.
  6. top with mayo and garnish, then serve hot!
